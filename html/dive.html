Diversity and inclusion


we’ve set an audacious goal: to make Link the most diverse, equitable, and inclusive workplace on the planet. And we’re not just setting high expectations for our own good. We’re aiming sky-high because we know from experience that reducing and eliminating inequity is hard to do if all you shoot for is incremental change.







Culture and belonging


Creating the most diverse and inclusive workplace on the planet won’t be possible if we settle for small, incremental changes. Realizing Link’s bold vision will require bold actions to match—starting with a complete shift in how Link works as a company. For us, that began with fundamentally transforming our culture. In the Self-ID survey, we asked Link employees around the world to voluntarily share with us how they identify, so we can better recognize and support the diverse populations in our workforce. In the year ahead, we plan to keep ramping up these and other investments in our people and culture. Whether by expanding our Employee Resource Groups—currently comprising more than a third of Link’s global employee population—or by extending our parental leave policy for all parents, regardless of gender or caregiver status, whether hourly or salaried, we won’t stop until Link is a workplace where everyone is encouraged and equipped to succeed.
